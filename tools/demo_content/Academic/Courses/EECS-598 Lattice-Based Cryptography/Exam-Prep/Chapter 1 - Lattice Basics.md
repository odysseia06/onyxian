# Chapter 1 - Lattice Basics

Studied 2025-12-28; PS1 attempted and written up the same day.

## Key Concepts

- A lattice is the integer span of a basis B; bases are equivalent up to unimodular transforms, so the determinant det(Λ) = |det B| is a lattice invariant — the volume of the fundamental parallelepiped.
- Successive minima λ₁ ≤ λ₂ ≤ … ≤ λₙ; Minkowski's first theorem gives λ₁ ≤ √n · det(Λ)^{1/n} — existence of short vectors, with a *non-constructive* proof, which is exactly the gap the whole field lives in.
- The dual lattice Λ* and the transference bounds; keeping primal/dual straight now pays off in the Gaussian chapters.
